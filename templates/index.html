Crisis Helplines

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (US): 1-800-273-TALK (8255)
  • Text HOME to 741741 (US): 24/7 free and confidential support for those in crisis.
  • Samaritans (UK): 116 123 - Available 24/7 for anyone in emotional distress.
  • Lifeline (Australia): 13 11 14 - 24/7 support for anyone in crisis.
  • Emergency Services: Dial 911 (US) or your local emergency number if you or someone else is in immediate danger.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is mental health?

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how people think, feel, and act. Mental health also influences how people cope with stress, relate to others, and make choices.

What are common signs of mental health issues?

Common signs include persistent feelings of sadness, anxiety, or hopelessness, difficulty concentrating, withdrawal from social activities, or changes in eating or sleeping patterns.

How can I manage my mental health?

Effective strategies include regular physical activity, a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, practicing mindfulness, and seeking support when needed from friends, family, or professionals.

When should I seek help from a professional?

If you are experiencing prolonged feelings of sadness, anxiety, or distress that interfere with daily life, it is important to reach out to a mental health professional for guidance and support.
